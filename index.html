PolInSAR Dashboards

Interactive, client-side reconstructions of Polarimetric SAR Interferometry papers and models — coherence optimization, temporal decorrelation, and forest structure inversion. Every plot is computed live in your browser; nothing is precomputed or uploaded.

→ Coherence optimization

Single-Baseline Polarimetric SAR Interferometry

Reconstructs the foundational Papathanassiou & Cloude (2001) result: the interferometric coherence of an RVoG scene depends on polarization only through the ground-to-volume ratio m(w), so the "optimum" coherences are exactly a generalized eigenvalue problem — solved here in closed form. Includes inversion-conditioning analysis (why a wide m-spectrum estimates height better) and a simulated tree-height validation.

K.P. Papathanassiou, S.R. Cloude, IEEE TGRS, vol. 39, no. 11, Nov. 2001.

 → Temporal decorrelation

Tropical Forest Canopy Height — Temporal Decorrelation Models

Runs all four real inversion algorithms from Luo et al. (2024) live — RVoG, RMoG, RVoG+VTD, and the paper's own faster single-loop RVoG+VTDs — showing exactly why repeat-pass temporal decorrelation makes a TD-blind model overestimate canopy height, and how each compensation strategy fixes it. Reproduces the paper's real accuracy/efficiency numbers from AfriSAR Gabon mangrove validation (up to 65 m canopy, 4602 LiDAR points).

H. Luo, C. Yue, Y. Wu, X. Zhang, C. Lu, G. Ou, Ecological Indicators 166 (2024) 112566.
