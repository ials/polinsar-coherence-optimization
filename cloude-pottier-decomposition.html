Cloude-Pottier Decomposition — Quad-Pol vs Dual-Pol SAR

An interactive, fully client-side reconstruction of the classical Cloude & Pottier (1997) H/α eigen-decomposition for quad-polarized [T3] data, side-by-side with the modified dual-polarization decomposition (HH-VV, HH-HV, HV-VV) derived by Ji & Wu (2015). Move the sliders to build a synthetic scattering-mixture (surface + dipole + dihedral + diffuse noise); every panel below — the H-α planes, the numeric comparison, and the synthetic zone-map scene — recomputes live from freshly simulated speckle (complex Gaussian, N-look averaging), exactly as a real multi-look SAR estimate would behave.

Theory: S.R. Cloude, E. Pottier, "An entropy based classification scheme for land applications of polarimetric SAR," IEEE TGRS 35(1), 1997. K. Ji, Y. Wu, "Scattering Mechanism Extraction by a Modified Cloude-Pottier Decomposition for Dual Polarization SAR," Remote Sens. 7(6), 7447-7470, 2015. Interaction style inspired by ashlinrichardson/cloude_decom (mouse-driven quad-pol target-cancellation GUI) — here re-imagined as slider-driven exploration of scattering mixtures, entropy, and the dual-pol scattering-mechanism degeneracy, with no data files or server required.

1 — Quad-Pol [T3] H-α Plane (Cloude & Pottier, 1997)

150 independent N-look Monte-Carlo estimates of the current mixture (entropy H uses log base 3, since q=3 channels). Solid curves: the classical, asymmetric full-pol feasible-region boundary (Eq. 7-8). Dashed lines: the standard 9-zone dividing lines (H = 0.5/0.9, α = 42.5°/47.5°). Markers: the three canonical scattering mechanisms, always at (0,0), (0,45), (0,90).

2 — Dual-Pol H-α Plane (Ji & Wu, 2015 — modified decomposition)

The same simulated looks, reduced to HH-VV, HH-HV, and HV-VV (q=2 channels, log base 2). All three share one symmetric boundary envelope (Eq. 20-21). HH-VV keeps the three mechanisms separated (as in Panel 1); HH-HV and HV-VV collapse them onto (or near) a single degenerate point, (H,α)=(0,0) — the paper's central finding.

4 — Retention Ratio (this simulation vs. Ji & Wu's real-data average)

From the synthetic scene below: % of pixels whose dual-pol zone label matches its quad-pol "ground-truth" zone (Rr, Eq. 36-37). Reference markers are Ji & Wu (2015), Table 3 — the average over 31×5 real datasets from six SAR sensors. 5 — Synthetic Scene: Scattering-Mechanism Zone Maps

Five fixed land-cover-like patches (calm water · vegetation · urban · mixed · rough/noisy), each pixel independently simulated at the current N. Left: quad-pol 9-zone classification (ground truth). Right: HH-HV modified classification — the only dual-pol mode NISAR's DHDH product actually provides. Zone numbering and colors follow Ji & Wu (2015), Figure 1 / Table 2 (Z2 and Z7 are structurally unattainable for HH-HV, per the inverted/unresolved dividing lines in their Table 2).

Scattering vectors. Pauli (quad-pol): k = [S_HH+S_VV, S_HH-S_VV, 2S_HV]/√2. Dual-pol reductions (Ji & Wu 2015, Eq. 10/17/19): HH-VV k=[S_HH+S_VV, S_HH-S_VV]/√2; HH-HV k=[S_HH, 2S_HV]; HV-VV k=[S_VV, 2S_HV]. Canonical targets (Eq. 22): surface diag(1,1), dipole diag(1,0), dihedral diag(1,-1) → for HH-HV all three give k=[1,0], i.e. identical (H,α)=(0,0) (Eq. 26); for HV-VV, surface and dihedral coincide at (0,0) while the dipole vector is exactly zero (undefined).
Entropy/alpha. λi = eigenvalues of ⟨T⟩=⟨kkH⟩; Pi=λi/Σλj; H = -ΣPilogqPi (q = number of channels: 3 for quad-pol, 2 for dual-pol); α = ΣPiαi, αi=arccos(|ui,1|). 3x3 eigen-decomposition uses the closed-form trigonometric solution for Hermitian matrices plus a cross-product eigenvector recovery; 2x2 uses the standard quadratic formula.
Boundary curves. Quad-pol: T1(m)=diag(1,m,m), T2(m)=diag(0,1,2m) for m≤0.5 else diag(2m-1,1,1), 0≤m≤1 (asymmetric, apex at H=1, α=60°). Dual-pol: T1(m)=diag(1,m), T2(m)=diag(m,1), 0≤m≤1 (symmetric about α=45°, apex at H=1, α=45°).
Mixture model. Each simulated look is drawn from one of surface / dipole / dihedral / diffuse-noise according to the sidebar's normalized weights; the first three are rank-1 (a complex Gaussian scalar times the mechanism's fixed unit Pauli vector), the noise term is a full isotropic complex Gaussian 3-vector. Averaging N such looks' outer products reproduces genuine finite-look entropy dispersion — the same mechanism that gives real multilook SAR estimates their Monte-Carlo scatter in the H-α plane. All computation runs client-side (no data leaves your browser); nothing here is fit to or loaded from any real SAR product.
